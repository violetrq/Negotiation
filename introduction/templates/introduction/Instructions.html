Experiment Instructions

This is an experiment in the economics of decision-making in online labor platforms such as Upwork and Freelancer. You will be randomly assigned as either a worker or recruiter. Please read all instructions carefully before proceeding.
Role Assignment and Experimental Structure
  • You will be randomly assigned as either a Worker or Recruiter
  • This role assignment is permanent throughout the experiment
  • The experiment consists of two phases:
    1. Performance evaluation phase
    2. Negotiation phase
Phase 1: Performance Evaluation Through Mathematical Task

Workers will be randomly matched with recruiters for a math proofreading task opportunity. Proofreaders must ensure accuracy and clarity of math exercises, examples, and solutions. Performance in the qualification task strongly correlates with math proofreading abilities.

For Workers

Performance evaluated against other Workers:

  • Highest performance: $1.50 contribution
  • Second-highest performance: $1.00 contribution
For Recruiters

Performance evaluated against one other Recruiter:

  • Highest performance: $2.50 contribution
  • Second-highest performance: $2.00 contribution

Note: In case of equal performance, contribution levels will be randomly determined.

Phase 2: Negotiation Process

After performance evaluation, each Worker will be randomly and anonymously paired with a Recruiter. The joint revenue equals the sum of both participants' contribution levels from Phase 1.

Worker Options
Option 1: Accept the Suggested Wage
  • Immediately concludes the economic portion
  • Worker receives the suggested wage
  • Recruiter receives remaining revenue (joint revenue minus suggested wage)
  • Both proceed to post-experimental survey
Option 2: Initiate Negotiation
  • Activates 3-minute negotiation period
  • Both parties access negotiation interface

Note: Recruiters do not need to make any choice at this stage.

Negotiation Rules and Procedures

Time Limit
  • You have 3 minutes to reach an agreement
  • A countdown timer will be displayed on your screen
  • The timer starts when both participants enter the negotiation phase
Communication Tools
  • Chat window for discussions
  • Wage proposal interface for submitting offers
Making and Accepting Proposals
  • Both parties can submit wage proposals at any time
  • Submit all proposals through the designated wage input box
  • All proposals will be visible to both parties
  • Only the most recent proposal from each party is valid
  • Click the "Accept" button next to a proposal to agree to it
Negotiation Results
If Agreement is Reached:
  • Worker receives: Agreed wage amount
  • Recruiter receives: Total revenue minus agreed wage
If No Agreement is Reached:
  • The suggested wage becomes the default
  • Both parties incur a $1.00 penalty
  • Worker receives: Suggested wage - $1.00
  • Recruiter receives: Remaining revenue - $1.00
Chat Guidelines
DO:
  • Keep all communication anonymous
  • Focus on wage negotiation
  • Maintain professional behavior
DO NOT:
  • Share personal information
  • Reveal your identity
  • Use inappropriate language
Breaking these rules may result in payment forfeiture.
Payment Details
Base Payment
  • All participants receive $1.50 for completing Phase 1 (math maze)
Additional Earnings
For Workers:
  • With agreement: Final negotiated wage
  • Without agreement: Suggested wage - $1.00
For Recruiters:
  • With agreement: Joint revenue - worker's wage
  • Without agreement: Joint revenue - suggested wage - $1.00
Important Protocols
  1. Read all screen instructions thoroughly
  2. Use 'OK' or 'Continue' buttons when prompted
  3. Stay at your device
  4. Use only the chat window and proposal interface on your screen for all communications

Note: All decisions and communications in this experiment are anonymous and will be used for research purposes only.
